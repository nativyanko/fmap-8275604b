THE PLAYBOOK - how winning funnels convert
1Belief before effort
The lifelong struggler's first question is not 'is this safe' or 'how much'. It is 'will this actually work for me, after everything I have already tried and failed'. So Voy front-loads belief: 100k users, named before-and-afters, Trustpilot, then a personalised projection built from your own numbers, all before a single hard question. And it bookends it, reinforcing the dream again at the assessment and even at checkout. People remember the peak and the ending, so Voy makes both of them the outcome. You earn the right to ask for data only after you have made them believe.
2It's not your fault
This is the emotional core for this customer. Years of failed diets have taught them to blame their own willpower. Voy reframes the problem from character to biology: how long have you struggled, what have you tried, what happens when you try to stay on track. Every answer quietly says the same thing: you did try, it was never enough, and that is not a moral failing, it is a mechanism willpower was never going to beat. Remove the shame and you remove the biggest objection of all, 'I should just try harder on my own'.
3Make them prescribe themselves
Voy never says 'you need this drug'. It pulls the pain out in the customer's own words (hunger, cravings, snacking, yo-yoing) and mirrors those exact words back on the results page as 'how the medication helps you'. Self-generated conclusions are unshakeable in a way a brand's claims never are. By the end the customer feels they diagnosed themselves and chose the solution, rather than being sold to.
4Order is engineered, not arbitrary
Easy, ego-friendly questions go first to build momentum and commitment, because each small yes makes the next more likely. The scary or boring parts, the long medical screen and the price, are deliberately pushed to the back half once the customer is invested and has already seen the dream. Nothing about the sequence is accidental.
5Personalisation is proof of care
'Treatment built around you', your numbers, your answers, a named Medical Director, a plan that adjusts to your dose. Every personalised touch does double duty: it makes the plan feel impossible to decline because it was built for them, and it signals real, careful medicine rather than an off-the-shelf product. For someone nervous about a serious medication, that feeling of being properly assessed is exactly what converts.
6De-risk every fear before you ask for the sale
Each objection that could stop a purchase is pre-handled before it can surface. Is it safe: clinicians and pharmacy badges. Will it work: a money-back guarantee. What about side effects: free clinician messaging. I hate needles: lead with the pill. By the time the price appears, every reason to say no has already been quietly answered.
7The default is the decision
Most people accept whatever is pre-selected. Voy pre-selects an answer on nearly every question to cut friction, and pre-selects a longer plan to lift lifetime value. Defaults are not a convenience, they are a lever. Bliss pre-selects nothing, adding friction on every step, and defaults to its worst, shortest plan.
8Never a bare price, always a basket
Voy never shows a naked number. It anchors (154 down to 79 for the first month), stacks value (app, tracking, clinician support, starter kit), adds a guarantee, and defers the ongoing cost until after commitment. The price always lands against a full, discounted, de-risked basket, so it reads as a deal rather than a spend.
9Maximise order value at peak intent (the upsell)
The most valuable instant in any funnel is the moment right after the customer says yes, when their guard is down and buying momentum is highest. Voy harvests it: the second you pick a plan it offers a Replenish supplement at 19/mo as a one-tap order-bump, shows it again in your cart at checkout, and surrounds the medication with a value stack of included extras. None of it adds friction to the core purchase, it just collects the intent already there. Bliss has no upsell anywhere, pure revenue left on the table at the cheapest moment to capture it.
10Win the switcher, not just new patients
A huge and fast-growing slice of this market is already on Mounjaro or Wegovy somewhere else, shopping for a better price or experience. The moment you say you have taken treatment before, Voy opens a dedicated branch: where did you last get it (logging which competitors it is winning from), which medication and dose, how recently, any side effects, then it pitches a safe switch to Wegovy with a head-to-head efficacy chart. That conquests rivals by name, leads with the cost-saving angle switchers care about, and reassures them the switch is clinically safe. Bliss does nothing when you say yes, treating a ready-to-buy switcher exactly like a nervous first-timer.
